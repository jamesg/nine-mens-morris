Nine Men's Morris

A Javascript implementation of the ancient strategy game for two players.

Rules

The game has two stages. In the first stage, players start with nine men each and take turns to place them on any unoccupied point. Three men of the same player placed along a straight line form a mill. When a player forms a mill, they may remove one of their opponent's men.

Once both players have placed all nine men, they take turns to move their own men one step at a time along the board lines. Whenever a player forms a mill, they may remove one man from their opponent.

Strategy

A good strategy is to place men along two parallel lines, so that you can form a mill on every turn by moving a man between the two lines.

Alternative Rules

Some versions of the game enter a third stage when one player is reduced to three men. The weaker player no longer needs to move men one space each turn, but may 'fly' to any unoccupied point on the board. Most rules stipulate that when removing an opponent's man, a man not in a mill should be selected if possible. This minor rule is omitted in this version.
